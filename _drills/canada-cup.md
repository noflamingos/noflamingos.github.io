title: "Canada Cup"
short_description: "Flow drill focusing on timing, skating routes, and giving and receiving passes in motion."
thumbnail: "/assets/images/drills/canada-cup/thumb.png"
images:
  - "/assets/images/drills/canada-cup/1.png"
setup: |
  Line up -the players in four groups at the red line on either centre of centre and at the top of the circle against the boards. Place pucks in opposite corners (on the same side as the players at the top of the circle). Players rotate through the different lines after each pass (X1 to X2, X2 to X3, X3 to X4, and X4 to X1). Have a coach line up at each group of pucks to start the drill.
full_description: |
  On the whistle, X2 and X4 start the drill skating their loop and receiving a pass from the coach. As this happens, the next X1/X3 skate around the offside face-off dots, pivot backwards and then forwards again, and receive a pass from X2/X4. They take the pass, skate on net, take a shot and then pick up a puck from behind the net to start the loop again.
coaching_points:
- Players should use their speed and skating path to put themselves in the correct position to receive a pass.
- Players must make exaggerated loops so they can receive the pass in stride skating up the ice without having to wait/slow down.
- Players should make accurate passes that lead the other players so they can receive passes in stride.
