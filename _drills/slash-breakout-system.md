setup: |
  We aim to breakout to the weak side of the ice after a D-to-D pass but this image shows the strong-side W receiving the breakout pass to simplify it.
full_description: |
  The breakout relies on the weak-side W "slashing" below their D and behind the strong side D skating up ice. The C provides low support to the strong-side W for a bump pass or support if the D pinches. If there is no pass to the C then the C fills the lane out wide.
  The strong-side W has several options depending on pressure from the forecheckers/D. They can chip the puck passed the D into the neutral zone, make a bump pass to the C providing low support, or make a direct pass to the weak-side W as they split the D skating up ice.
coaching_points:
- Strong-side W has to read pressure for the opposing D prior to receiving the pass and make the correct decision based on if the D is pinching or not.
- C has to stay below the puck to be able to receive a pass from the strong-side W. If they skate passed the W they cannot receive a pass if the D pinches and prevents it.
- Weak-side W must time their slash based on what the strong-side W is doing. If there is a pass to the middle of the ice they need to be in the right lane. If the W is chipping the puck out they need to be ready to accelerate and meet the puck in the NZ.
- All three F need to fill lanes moving up the ice to become F1, F2, and F3.
